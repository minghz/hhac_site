Tournaments

Shooting in an archery tournament is an exciting way to challenge your abilities, both physical and mental, and learn surprisingly a lot about yourself. The HHAC is dedicated to promoting a fun and sportsmanlike environment for archers of all skill levels and shooting styles. There are competetive categories for instinctive, novice recurve (using a sight), Olympic Recurve, and Open/Compound divisions.

HHAC members also participate in external tournaments against other clubs and at summer events such as the Spring Classic. Every February, HHAC hosts the annual Inter-University Archery Tournament (IUAT)! Archers from UofT, York, Queens, and Waterloo compete against each other in the various categories.

In-house tournaments

We host several tournaments right in the range throughout the school year:

  • The Halloween Fun Shoot at the end of October brings costumes, candy, swinging pumpkins, and zombies. There's lots of candy to be had too.
  • The Winter Tournament happens the last week of classes and is a more serious event using FITA targets and scoring rules. It is a great chance to test your skills in a more formal setting.
  • The Valentine's Day Fun Shoot in mid-February is filled with chocolate for eating, balloons for shooting, and awkward themed prizes!
  • The final event each year is the Club Championship. The final test of skills, similar to the Winter Tournament, using FITA targets and rules. Winners for each category are awarded a medal and eternal glory!

Dates of upcoming events are posted on Facebook.
